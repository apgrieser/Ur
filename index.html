Rules

Goal: Move all your pieces through your game board path before your opponent does the same for their path

  • Play alternates between players after each dice roll, with exceptions noted below
  • A player rolls the dice by pressing the roll button; each die will either present a dot (1) or no dot (0) - the dice roll is the sum of the four dice
  • A player moves one piece the number rolled (rolls may not be divided among multiple pieces); the game board shows possible moves - click/tap on the space to move there
  • A piece may move to any unoccupied space in the player's path
  • If the player moves a piece to a rosette (gold star space), the player gets another turn
  • A player may not move to a rosette occupied by an opponent's piece
  • A player may move to any other non-rosette space occupied by the opponent along their path
  • If a player's piece lands on a space occupied by the opponent's piece (and the space is not a rosette), the opponent's piece is sent back to start
  • If a player rolls a zero, no pieces move and play passes to the opponent
  • If a player has no possible moves for the number rolled, play passes to the opponent
  • You must roll the exact number to move your piece off the board to its home position

Settings:

  • Play Computer (random):  Computer move chosen randomly from the set of possible moves
  • Play Computer (traditional):  Computer move chosen from possible moves using logic based on game rules, such as moving to a rosette or sending opponent home (default play mode)
  • Play Computer (simulations):  Computer move chosen from possible moves by simulating possible look ahead moves and choosing the one with the best outcome
  • Play A Human (local):  Two humans sitting side by side can play each other
